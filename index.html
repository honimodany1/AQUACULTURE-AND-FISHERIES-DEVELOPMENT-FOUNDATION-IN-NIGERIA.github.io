INTRODUCTION

The name aquaculture and fisheries may be new to some people but the activities of aquaculture are not. Most people know aquaculture by the name ‘FISH FARMING”, which is correct. In the actual sense, aquaculture (also under water agriculture) is the cultivation and harvesting of animals and plants such as finfish, shellfish and sea weeds in the water Fisheries at the other hand are the harvesting of the water animals from the wild or from aquaculture. It could be fresh water, marine water or brackish water. Aquaculture and Fisheries development is all about making nutritious food available for everyone to fight hunger and malnutrition. This can only be achieved on a sustainable basis by the involvement of individuals, families, communities, organizations and the government. Medical research into the health benefits of eating fish is that Omega-3 fatty acids, which are typically found in most fish, help reduce many forms of cancer and promote healthy brain tissue. Eating fish regularly has also been shown to reduce the risk of heart disease through reducing the probability of clot formation, lowering blood pressure and increasing the good cholesterol levels in the blood stream.

Development

1) Consensual aquaculture and fisheries development policy between the government and the farmers/fishers shall be encouraged and fostered.
2). Training farmers and fishers on the modern methods, tools and techniques shall be our focus through seminars, workshops, farm tours and conferences ( onsite and online), shall be our regular features to get them updated with fast trend of development in the global market.
3) Membership registration into the AFDFIN shall be open to individuals and corporate bodies to be benefited by the strategic plans.
4). Members shall be given opportunities to benefit from the agricultural loans from the government and other corporate social financial aid.
5) We shall promote and facilitate the establishment of hatcheries and fish feed manufacturing to aid the farmers and to ensure quality fish seed and feed.
6) Crisis intervention centers shall be established in every geopolitical region to help reduce the losses in farm production and folding up of established farms.
7) We shall liaise with farm tools and equipment manufacturing companies to broker essential instruments to ease members in purchasing essential instruments.
8) We shall engaged in the seafood ventures by promoting and fostering marine aquaculture and fisheries (mariculture).
9) Fish stocking in some waters that overfishing and oil spillage has extinct some kind fish populations, shall be a regular activities.
10) Collaborations and networking with foreign aquacultural and fisheries experts shall be conducted as often as possible to enhance our values in technology transfer.
11) Inland fresh water aquaculture will be focused immensely as the country is blessed with abundant inland water ecosystem.

FROM THE CEO'S DESK

Aquaculture without a strategic planning, including research cannot yield dividend. In Nigeria, we fail in aquaculture because there is no adequate planning, from the government to the fish farmers. Again, most fish farms are operating as backyard fish farms. Aquaculture and Fisheries Development Foundation in Nigeria is coming up with something different. We are going to divide aquaculture investment into two systems based on the aquatic ecosystems in Nigeria.
1) The marine and brackish waters. This will include the Atlantic Ocean and deltas.
2) The inland fresh waters. This will include inland water ways,
the Niger and the Benue basins, all the wetlands and Lake Chad. In the marine and brackish waters, the following supporting equipments will be used:
1) Submersible cages and other highly technological methods to into Open Ocean fish farming.
2) National hatcheries for finfish and shellfish.
3)Feed mills.
4) Post harvest centers.
5) Research and disease control centers. In the inland freshwaters, the technique that is going to be used will be determined by the fish expert consultants. Again, the following supporting equipments will be employed:
1) Hatcheries
2) Feed mills
3) Post harvest centers
4) Research and disease control centers.I want to encourage the Nigeria populace to buy the culture of team working, come together and teamwork for the success of aquaculture in Nigeria. The fish farmers and upcoming farmers are encouraged to join by registering with AFDFIN. Finally, I call on the government, companies whose corporate social responsibility are to help in a pathetic situation that we find ourselves in Nigeria, eminent Patriots, fish farmers, fishers, and the entire people living in Nigeria to come and join hands together, and make Nigeria a for nations. Foreign investmentors are welcome. Peace and unity is promised our great country, the great pillar of Africa, Nigeria.
